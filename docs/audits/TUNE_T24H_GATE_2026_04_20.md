## Mechanism of the PnL uplift

The **+15 SOL PnL gain over pro-rated baseline** comes from **TRAILING_STOP capturing 44% more trades with comparable avg PnL**, not from the modeled `stop_loss_35%` near-miss reduction (which under-performed target).

| Path | Count Δ | PnL Δ |
|---|---:|---:|
| TRAILING_STOP conversion | +54 trades (22.77h) | +23.8 SOL (vs pro-rated 59.7) |
| `stop_loss_35%` reduction | -10 trades (vs pro-rated 38.3) | +2.9 SOL saved (10 × avg -0.28 SOL) |
| `no_momentum_90s` reduction | -12 trades | -1.9 SOL (worse per-trade) |
| `max_extended_hold` shift | -0 net (ratio similar) | -5 SOL (path restructured) |

The +15 SOL uplift is dominated by TRAILING_STOP *converting* peaks that would otherwise have been `max_extended_hold` or `time_exit` — not by converting near-miss `stop_loss_35%` events. The tune's thesis ("lower trail activation catches peaks before they stop-out at -35%") is PARTIALLY correct but the actual uplift pathway is different from the prediction.

**Implication:** watch whether the `TRAILING_STOP` / `max_extended_hold` conversion holds in the 2nd 24h window. If the capture gets saturated (TRAILING_STOP count stops growing relative to pro-rated baseline), the uplift thesis weakens.
